Safe Sex Practices

Practicing safe sex is important for maintaining sexual health and overall well-being. It involves taking measures to prevent sexually transmitted infections (STIs), unwanted pregnancies, and other health risks associated with sexual activity.

Proper Usage of Contraceptives


Condom


  • Use a new condom for each act of intercourse.

  • Check the expiration date and ensure the package is not damaged.
  • Open the package carefully to avoid tearing the condom.

    • Birth Control Pills


  • Take the pill at the same time every day.li>
  • Follow the specific instructions for your pill type (some require a break week, others don't).
  • Use a backup method if you miss a pill or take it late (consult the pill's instructions for specifics).

    • Intrauterine Devices


  • Have the IUD inserted by a healthcare professional.
  • Check the strings of the IUD periodically to ensure it is in place.
  • Follow up with your healthcare provider as recommended.


    • Learn to communicate with your partner


    - Discuss sexual limitations, preferences, and safe sex practices in an open and sincere manner. Before engaging in sexual activity, disclose your STI status and ask your partner(s) to do the same.


    Regular Testing for STI (Sexually Transmitted Infections)

  • Get STI testing done on a regular basis, particularly if you're starting a new relationship or have several partners. Urge your partner or partners to take the test as well.

  • By following these tips, you can significantly reduce your risk of STI'S unintended pregnancies and other sexual health issues, ensuring a healthier and more enjoyable sexual experience
