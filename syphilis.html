Risk Factors

Engaging in sexual activity involves various risks, including sexually transmitted infections (STIs), unintended pregnancies, and emotional and psychological impacts. Understanding these risks is essential for making informed decisions and taking precautions to ensure a healthy and responsible approach to sexual relationships.

Syphilis


This is a sexually transmitted infection caused by the bacterium Treponema palladium, a spirochete. If left untreated, syphilis may progress through four phases.


  • Primary (chancre sore appear)

  • Secondary (general skin rashes occur)

  • Latent (a period that can last for several years with no overt symptoms)

  • Tertiary (cardiovascular disease, blindness, paralysis, skin ulcers, mental problem and even death occur).
